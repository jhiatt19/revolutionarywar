The Iriquois nation, also known as the 6 nations, comprised of the Mohawk, Oneida, Ondondaga, Cayuga, Seneca, and Tuscarora. When fighting first broke out, American diplomats were able to establish peace and urged for the Iriquois to stay neutral during the war. On July 13, 1775 the contential congress drafted a speech to the Six Nations explaining the history between the British and the American colonists and asking for the Iriquois to stay neutral while the colonists and British worked out there grievances.
